Our Story — a welcome from our founder

In 2015, Tim — then a trained Early Years Educator looking for the right environment for his own daughter — opened Nido Montessori Barcelona, the first Montessori centre active in the city since 1924. What began with 8 children in a 130 m² space grew to over 40 within three years. Barcelona Montessori School opened in September 2020. Today we serve 195+ children and their families across two campuses, over 3,000 m² of installations and 6,000 m² of land.

Montessori Teacher Training

AMS-affiliated adolescent training, hosted at BMS in Barcelona.

Barcelona Montessori School is home to the Barcelona Montessori Training Center, created to bring high-quality Montessori adolescent teacher training to Europe. The centre offers an AMS Secondary I–II pathway for ages 12–18 in partnership with CMStep, the Cincinnati Montessori Secondary Teacher Education Program.

The programme is designed as a low-residency model for working teachers: online coursework is combined with in-person training periods in Barcelona. Training is delivered in English, with bilingual instructors present, and assignments may be submitted in Spanish.

What the programme covers

Philosophy, adolescent development and practical classroom leadership.

The syllabus includes Montessori philosophy and adolescent development, prepared learning environments, student independence and interdependence, community building, meaningful thematic curriculum, outdoor and land-based experiences, service learning, field studies, practical life for adolescence, appropriate use of technology, and authentic assessment.

Why BMS hosts it

Supporting the growth of Montessori secondary education in Europe.

BMS chose this pathway because the school is AMS verified and wants its adolescent team to hold recognised Montessori credentials. The training also supports a city-based model of Montessori adolescence, using Barcelona and the surrounding community as resources for study and work.

Curriculum & Enrichment

Montessori depth,
recognised standards.

Our curriculum is developmental rather than age-driven — and fully integrated with the frameworks families need for continuity and recognition anywhere in the world.

The Montessori curriculum

Each environment follows the full Montessori scope and sequence for its plane of development — from sensorial and practical life in the Children's House to Cosmic Education in Primary and land, enterprise and community work in the Adolescent Community.

Integrated frameworks

The Montessori curriculum is mapped to the Early Years Foundation Stage and the English National Curriculum, and delivers Catalan and Spanish language and literature as required for full homologation — so learning records are recognised by British, Spanish and Catalan authorities alike.

Assessment that supports growth

Progress is evidenced through observation, portfolios, projects and reflective dialogue — mastery over marks — with selective, age-appropriate formal assessment as students move towards externally recognised qualifications.

Specialist enrichment

Dedicated specialists in Catalan, Spanish, Art, Music, PE and Special Educational Needs work across Early Years, Primary and Secondary — alongside a rich programme of extracurricular activities and the summer camp.

Community projects

Learning connects to the real world through long-running community partnerships — including Fundació Ánima, VHIO and the Race Against Hunger — and through student-led initiatives such as Montessori Model United Nations.

Support for every learner

Diverse learners are supported through differentiation, accommodations and Individual International Learning Plans, with a Special Educational Needs framework built on early identification and collaborative review with families.

Carrer Pomaret 25
Outdoor Learning

6,000 m² of garden — a curriculum in itself.

The Mas Pomaret is one of the oldest Masies in Sarrià. The remaining 6,000 m² of heritage garden surrounds our Early Years and Secondary campuses. Children grow food, observe nature, and spend real time outdoors every day — not as a break from learning, but as the deepest kind of it.

For our Secondary students, agriculture and land-based work are woven into the programme. There is no other urban school in Barcelona — or barely anywhere — with a space like this.

Community

A genuinely international, multilingual community.

English is the language that unites our community of 40+ nationalities. Children absorb Spanish and Catalan as a matter of daily life — not formal instruction. Communication is natural, and we celebrate the richness that other languages and cultures bring to our understanding of the world.

BMS is a micro-society. Children learn to navigate complexity, resolve conflict, and support one another in a real, mixed-age community — skills that no classroom exercise can replace.

Secondary, the Montessori way.

Opened in September 2025 and growing to 18 cohort by cohort, our Adolescent programme is built around what this age actually needs: real work, real responsibility, and a real community. Parent Involvement

Three ways families shape school life.

Community at BMS is built by parents, for everyone. Three connected initiatives make it easy to take part — whatever your time, language or interests.

FAB CLUB

Families Association of BMS

A non-profit, secular and apolitical association run by parents to build a warm, connected school community. The FAB Club organises social events, projects and activities, provides volunteers for school events, and fundraises so that every student can join school trips regardless of financial circumstances. It is committed to equality, diversity and accessibility, with democratic and transparent governance.

  • Informal meetings: 3rd Tuesday of the month, 9:15–10:30 in the school canteen
  • Initiatives include penpal projects, Community Week, monthly "park & cake", parent education events and weekend family activities
  • Open to all families — a welcoming way to meet like-minded parents across classes

Note: the FAB Club is not a representative body in discussions with the school — that role belongs to the Parent Representatives.

AMBASSADORS

Family Ambassador Programme

Current families welcome newly arrived families — especially those relocating internationally — through their first months at the school and in Barcelona. Families are matched by the age of their children, languages spoken at home, and shared interests.

  • New families are automatically offered an ambassador contact when they join
  • A friendly way to meet new families and build community spirit
  • Current families are warmly invited to sign up
PARENT REPS

Parent Representative Scheme

Each class has a designated Parent Representative who liaises directly with the school on behalf of parents — the formal channel for parent suggestions and ideas. Developed in consultation with teaching staff and the FAB Club, the scheme has an agreed job description and a transparent application and voting procedure.

  • One representative per class, liaising directly with the school
  • Clear job description and democratic voting procedure
  • The school's formal route for parent feedback and initiatives
